Geography & Climate Zones

Unique isolation: Andes Mountains (east), Pacific Ocean (west), Atacama Desert (north), Patagonia (south)

Result: Phylloxera-free (never arrived due to isolation). Ungrafted vines common.

Three climate zones:

  • Costa (Coast): Cool, Pacific influence, fog, high acidity wines
  • Entre Cordilleras (Between mountain ranges): Moderate, most production here
  • Andes: High altitude, cooler temperatures, intense sunlight, diurnal range

üçá Chilean Carmen√®re - The Signature Grape

History: Originally from Bordeaux (pre-phylloxera), thought extinct. Rediscovered in Chile 1990s (misidentified as Merlot for decades).

Style: Full body, medium to high tannin, medium to high acidity, deep color. Signature flavor: Herbaceous green pepper, bell pepper (when under-ripe), black fruit, chocolate, coffee (when ripe).

Best regions: Colchagua Valley, Cachapoal Valley (warm sites)

Aging: 5-10 years for quality examples

üçá Argentine Malbec - World-Class Signature

Origin: Originally from Cahors, France (minor Bordeaux variety). Thrives in Argentina's high-altitude conditions.

Style: Full body, soft velvety tannins (signature texture), high alcohol (14-15%), deep purple color, ripe plum, black cherry, blackberry, violet, chocolate, coffee. Approachable young but ages well.

Why Argentina? Altitude provides balance - intense sun ripens fruit fully while cool nights preserve acidity. UV radiation = thick skins = deep color and soft tannins.

Quality levels:

  • Value Malbec: Fruity, soft, drink young (2-5 years)
  • Premium Malbec: Structured, complex, oak-aged, ages 5-15 years
  • Top Malbec: World-class, concentrated, age-worthy 10-20+ years

International reputation: Argentine Malbec has become a global benchmark, rivaling Cabernet and Pinot Noir in prestige.
