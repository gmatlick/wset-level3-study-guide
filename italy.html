Barolo DOCG

Grape: Nebbiolo 100%

Style: Full body, high tannin, high acidity, pale garnet color, tar, roses, dried herbs

Winemaking: Extended maceration, traditionally large oak botti (some modern producers use barriques), 38 months minimum aging (18 in wood)

Aging: 10-30+ years. Riserva requires 62 months total aging.

Key villages: La Morra (elegant), Serralunga d'Alba (powerful), Barolo village, Castiglione Falletto

Barbaresco DOCG

Grape: Nebbiolo 100%

Style: Similar to Barolo but lighter, more elegant, approachable younger due to lower altitude and warmer mesoclimate

Aging: 26 months minimum (9 in wood). Riserva 50 months. Ages 8-20 years typically.

Other Piedmont Wines

Barbera d'Asti DOCG: High acidity, low tannin, cherry fruit, medium body, drink young (3-7 years) or age in oak for complexity

Dolcetto d'Alba DOC: Soft tannins, low acidity, purple fruit, drink young (2-5 years)

Gavi DOCG: Cortese grape, crisp, lemon, green apple, high acidity, drink young (1-3 years)

Asti DOCG: Sweet, sparkling Moscato. Low alcohol (5-6%), aromatic, peach, grape flavors

Tuscany (Toscana)

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Warm Mediterranean, maritime influence on coast, continental inland, hot dry summers

ü™® Soil

Galestro (schist), alberese (limestone), clay, varied altitude 150-500m

üçá Key Grape

Sangiovese dominates (many clones), plus international varieties (Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah)

Chianti DOCG

Grape: Sangiovese minimum 70%, can blend with Canaiolo, international varieties

Style: Medium body, high acidity, high tannin, red cherry, dried herbs, tobacco

Aging: Drink young (2-5 years) to medium aging (5-10 years depending on quality)

Chianti Classico DOCG

Grape: Sangiovese minimum 80%

Style: Higher quality than basic Chianti, more structured, better aging potential

Winemaking: Oak aging common, can use barriques or botti

Aging: Normale (2-8 years), Riserva requires 24 months (8-15+ years), Gran Selezione (top tier, 10-20 years)

Brunello di Montalcino DOCG

Grape: Sangiovese (Brunello clone) 100%

Climate: Warmer, drier than Chianti, south of Siena

Style: Full body, powerful, concentrated, very high tannin and acidity, red cherry, leather, tobacco, earthy

Aging: Minimum 5 years before release (2 in oak). Riserva 6 years. Ages 15-30+ years.

Vino Nobile di Montepulciano DOCG

Grape: Sangiovese (Prugnolo Gentile clone) minimum 70%

Style: Between Chianti Classico and Brunello in power/structure

Aging: 2 years minimum. Riserva 3 years. Ages 8-15 years.

Bolgheri DOC (Super Tuscans)

Location: Coastal Tuscany, maritime influence

Grapes: Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, some Sangiovese

Style: Full body, ripe fruit, oak influence, Bordeaux-like but riper. International style.

Famous wines: Sassicaia, Ornellaia

Aging: 8-20 years depending on producer

Toscana IGT

Allows flexibility for producers. Super Tuscans that don't follow DOCG rules. Can use international varieties, different aging, innovative techniques.

Veneto

Valpolicella DOC / Valpolicella Classico DOC

Grapes: Corvina (dominant), Rondinella, Corvinone

Climate: Moderate, Lake Garda influence, foothills of Alps

Style: Light to medium body, high acidity, low to medium tannin, fresh cherry, herbal notes

Aging: Drink young (1-4 years)

Amarone della Valpolicella DOCG

Production: Appassimento method - grapes dried for 3-4 months, concentrates sugars/flavors, fermented dry (or nearly dry)

Style: Full body, high alcohol (15-16%), dried fruit, chocolate, tobacco, prune, fig. Rich, intense, complex.

Aging: Minimum 2 years. Riserva 4 years. Ages 10-25+ years.

Recioto della Valpolicella DOCG

Production: Same appassimento method as Amarone but fermentation stopped early, leaving residual sugar

Style: Sweet red wine, full body, high alcohol, dried fruit, chocolate. Rare style.

Valpolicella Ripasso DOC

Production: Valpolicella wine refermented on Amarone lees, adding body/flavor/alcohol

Style: Between Valpolicella and Amarone - medium to full body, richer than basic Valpolicella

Aging: 5-10 years

Soave DOC / Soave Classico DOC

Grape: Garganega (minimum 70%)

Style: Light to medium body, high acidity, lemon, green apple, almond. Simple, fresh, affordable.

Soave Superiore DOCG: Higher quality, more concentration, potential for oak aging, better aging (3-7 years)

Pinot Grigio delle Venezie DOC / Veneto IGT

Grape: Pinot Grigio (Pinot Gris)

Style: Light body, high acidity, simple, neutral, citrus, pear. Huge volume production, drink young (1-2 years)

Other Important Italian Regions

Alto Adige / Trentino

Climate: Alpine influence, cool
Wines: Pinot Grigio, Pinot Bianco, Chardonnay (whites), Schiava, Lagrein (reds). Fresh, aromatic, high quality.

Friuli-Venezia Giulia (Collio, Colli Orientali, Friuli Grave)

Wines: High-quality whites (Pinot Grigio, Friulano, Sauvignon Blanc). Orange wines (skin contact). Fresh, mineral, aromatic.

Marche - Verdicchio dei Castelli di Jesi DOC

Grape: Verdicchio. Style: Medium body, high acidity, lemon, fennel, almond. Good aging potential (3-8 years).

Umbria - Orvieto DOC

Grapes: Grechetto, Trebbiano. Style: Dry whites, light, fresh, neutral. Drink young.

Lazio - Frascati DOC

Grapes: Malvasia, Trebbiano. Style: Dry white, light, simple, drink young.

Abruzzo - Montepulciano d'Abruzzo DOC

Grape: Montepulciano. Style: Medium body, soft tannins, cherry, plum. Good value, drink young to medium aging (3-8 years).

Campania

Taurasi DOCG: Aglianico grape, full body, high tannin, ages well (10-20 years)
Fiano di Avellino DOCG: Fiano grape, medium body, stone fruit, honey
Greco di Tufo DOCG: Greco grape, citrus, mineral

Puglia

Hot southern region
Grapes: Negroamaro, Primitivo (Zinfandel)
Style: Full body, high alcohol, ripe fruit, modern and traditional styles

Basilicata - Aglianico del Vulture DOC

Grape: Aglianico. Style: Full body, high tannin, volcanic soils, black fruit, earthy. Ages 8-15 years.

Sicily (Sicilia)

Nero d'Avola: Full body, plum, chocolate
Etna DOC: Nerello Mascalese (red), Carricante (white), volcanic slopes, high quality, mineral
