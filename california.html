üèõÔ∏è US Wine Laws & Labeling

AVA: American Viticultural Area - defines geographic boundaries only (not grape varieties, yields, or winemaking practices like European appellations)

Labeling rules:

  • If AVA stated: 85% of grapes must be from that AVA
  • If single vineyard stated: 95% of grapes from that vineyard
  • If vintage stated: 95% from that vintage
  • If varietal stated: 75% minimum of that variety (85% for Oregon)

California Overview

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Mediterranean - warm, dry summers with little rainfall during growing season. Wide range from cool (coastal) to hot (inland valleys).

üåä Key Factor

Pacific Ocean influence: Cold California Current creates fog and moderates temperature in coastal areas. Valleys perpendicular to coast channel cool air inland.

üçá Main Varieties

Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Zinfandel, Merlot, Sauvignon Blanc

General Style Characteristics

California wines typically: Full body, high alcohol (14-15.5% common), ripe fruit, oak influence (new French oak common for premium wines), fruit-forward, bold, powerful style

Napa Valley AVA

The most prestigious wine region in the USA. Small in area but huge in reputation. Premium, expensive wines.

üìç Location

North of San Francisco, north-south valley, 30 miles long, 1-5 miles wide

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Warm to hot, but cooling ocean influence in south (San Pablo Bay). Significant diurnal range. Morning fog in cooler areas.

ü™® Soils

Diverse - volcanic, gravel, clay. Well-drained hillside sites premium.

üç∑ Napa Cabernet Sauvignon Style

Full body, high alcohol (14-15.5%), high but ripe tannins, concentrated black fruit (cassis, blackberry, black cherry), new French oak (vanilla, toast, cedar), powerful, structured, age-worthy (10-30+ years for best wines).

Quality level: Among the world's finest Cabernet. Commands premium prices. Cult wines fetch thousands per bottle.

Key Sub-AVAs of Napa Valley

Stags Leap District

Location: Eastern benchland, south Napa

Famous for: Cabernet Sauvignon - elegant, silky tannins, balance. Less powerful than other Napa Cab but refined. (1976 Judgment of Paris winner from here)

Oakville

Location: Mid-valley, valley floor

Style: Classic Napa Cabernet - powerful, concentrated, structured. Famous estates: Opus One, Screaming Eagle

Rutherford

Location: Mid-valley, valley floor

Characteristic: "Rutherford Dust" - distinctive dusty, earthy tannin character in Cabernet. Powerful, structured wines.

Howell Mountain

Location: High elevation (1400-2200 ft), eastern mountains

Style: Very powerful, tannic, concentrated Cabernet. Above fog line = more sun, thicker skins. Needs long aging (15-30 years).

Mount Veeder

Location: Western mountains, high elevation

Style: Powerful, structured, tannic Cabernet. Mountain character.

Los Carneros (overlaps Sonoma)

Location: South Napa, San Pablo Bay cooling

Speciality: Coolest part of Napa. Pinot Noir & Chardonnay (not Cabernet). Sparkling wine production (traditional method). Elegant, high acid styles.

Saint Helena

Location: Mid to north valley

Style: Full-bodied, concentrated Cabernet. Warm site.

Calistoga

Location: North end of valley

Climate: Warmest part of Napa, least fog influence

Style: Very ripe, full-bodied, high alcohol Cabernet and Zinfandel

Sonoma County

Larger and more diverse than Napa. Greater maritime influence overall = cooler. More variety in wine styles and price points. Contains multiple distinct AVAs.

Russian River Valley AVA

Climate: Cool, fog from Pacific via Petaluma Gap and Russian River. Morning fog, afternoon sun.

Speciality: World-class Pinot Noir and Chardonnay

Pinot Noir style: Medium body, elegant, high acidity, red fruit (cherry, raspberry, cranberry), earthy, mushroom. Less oaky than Napa Cab, more restrained, Burgundian approach. Age 5-15 years.

Chardonnay style: Medium to full body, citrus, stone fruit (peach, nectarine), oak influence, balanced. Quality rivals Burgundy.

Sonoma Coast AVA

Climate: Very cool, coastal influence, fog

Wines: Pinot Noir, Chardonnay. Even more elegant and restrained than Russian River. High acidity, mineral character.

Dry Creek Valley AVA

Climate: Warmer, inland, less fog

Speciality: Zinfandel (old vines)

Style: Full body, high alcohol (14.5-16%), jammy red and black fruit, spice, sometimes rustic. Can be very powerful.

Also: Good Sauvignon Blanc (fresh, herbaceous)

Alexander Valley AVA

Climate: Warmer inland valley

Wines: Cabernet Sauvignon (full-bodied, ripe, structured), Merlot, Zinfandel. Less refined than Napa but good quality, better value.

Central Coast Regions

Santa Cruz Mountains AVA

Location: Mountains south of San Francisco Bay

Elevation: High altitude (400-800m), above fog

Wines: Cabernet Sauvignon, Pinot Noir, Chardonnay. Elegant, structured, age-worthy. Small production, premium quality.

Santa Maria Valley AVA (Santa Barbara County)

Unique feature: East-west oriented valley (unusual) - funnels cool Pacific air directly inland

Climate: Cool, long growing season, high diurnal range

Wines: Pinot Noir, Chardonnay. Elegant, balanced, high quality. Similar to Russian River but slightly warmer.

Sta. Rita Hills AVA (Santa Barbara County)

Climate: Cool, coastal

Speciality: Pinot Noir - elegant, complex, age-worthy

Other California Regions

Mendocino County

North of Sonoma. Cool (Anderson Valley - Pinot, Chard, sparkling) to warm inland areas. Organic/sustainable viticulture focus.

Sierra Foothills

Gold Rush area, old-vine Zinfandel. High elevation, warm days, cool nights. Rustic, powerful reds.

Lodi AVA

Central Valley, warm. Old-vine Zinfandel specialist. Historically bulk wine, but quality focus increasing. Good value wines.

Central Valley

Hot, inland, high-volume production. Mostly inexpensive wines for domestic market. Irrigation essential.
