Diverse terroirs and maritime climate excellence

Overview

Most of South Africa's wine regions are found on and around the coastline of the Western Cape. The combination of maritime climate and complex mountain topography enables South African winemakers to produce a broad range of wine styles.

Strong south-easterly wind in spring/summer

Grape Varieties

White Grape Varieties

üçá Chenin Blanc - South Africa's Signature White

Most planted white variety in South Africa

Locally known as "Steen"

Versatility:

  • Range from simple, fruity to complex, age-worthy
  • Dry to sweet styles
  • Still and sparkling wines

Quality Dry Chenin Blanc:

  • Fresh stone fruit (peach, apricot)
  • Citrus and tropical notes
  • High acidity
  • Can develop honey, toast with age
  • Often oak-aged for premium wines

Sauvignon Blanc

Second most planted white variety

Styles:

  • Cool regions: Crisp, herbaceous, green fruit
  • Warmer regions: Riper, tropical fruit
  • Often unoaked for varietal purity
  • Some producers use oak for texture and complexity

Key Regions:

Elgin, Constantia, Cape South Coast

Chardonnay

Planted in cooler regions for quality production

Styles: From crisp, unoaked to rich, barrel-fermented

Best from: Walker Bay, Elgin, Hemel-en-Aarde

Cabernet Sauvignon

Most planted black variety in South Africa

Characteristics:

  • Full-bodied, high tannins
  • Blackcurrant, cedar, tobacco
  • Often blended in Bordeaux-style blends
  • Excellent aging potential

Best from: Stellenbosch

Syrah/Shiraz

Increasingly important in South Africa

Styles: From elegant, peppery cool-climate to rich, full-bodied warm-climate

Best from: Swartland, Stellenbosch

Other Important Varieties

Merlot: Often used in Bordeaux blends

Pinot Noir: Cooler regions - Walker Bay, Hemel-en-Aarde

Grenache: Old-vine Grenache gaining recognition, especially in Swartland

South African Wine Laws

Wine of Origin (W.O.) Scheme

South Africa's geographical indication system

W.O. on Label Guarantees:

  • Geographic origin
  • Vintage (if stated)
  • Variety (if stated)

Minimum Requirements:

  • 85% of grapes from stated region
  • 85% from stated vintage
  • 85% from stated variety

W.O. Geographic Hierarchy

Geographical Unit (largest)

Example: Western Cape - encompasses most South African wine regions

Over 90% of SA wine production

Region

Example: Coastal Region

Large area encompassing multiple districts

District

Examples: Stellenbosch, Paarl, Franschhoek

More specific geographical areas

Ward (smallest)

Most specific designation

Examples: Hemel-en-Aarde Valley, Elim

Single vineyard names may also appear

Estate Wines

Estate: Wine must be from grapes grown, made, and bottled on a single property

Indicates vertical integration and control over entire production process

Key Wine Regions

Coastal Region

Large region encompassing several important districts. Dominated by maritime climate influence.

Stellenbosch District

Most prestigious wine district in South Africa

Geography:

  • Stretches from coast inland to foothills
  • Variety of altitudes and aspects
  • Diverse soil types

Climate:

  • Generally warm
  • Cooling influence from False Bay
  • Altitude provides cooling in mountain areas

Specialties:

  • Cabernet Sauvignon - flagship variety
  • Bordeaux-style blends
  • Also: Merlot, Pinotage, Syrah
  • Sauvignon Blanc and Chardonnay from cooler sites

Paarl District

North of Stellenbosch

Climate:

Generally warmer than Stellenbosch - less maritime influence

Specialties:

  • Full-bodied reds: Cabernet, Shiraz, Pinotage
  • Chenin Blanc - both simple and premium styles
Franschhoek Ward (within Paarl)

Surrounded by mountains

Known for quality Cap Classique (traditional method sparkling)

Constantia District (Cape Town)

Historic wine region - South Africa's oldest

Location: Southern peninsula near Cape Town

Climate:

  • Cool - closest to ocean
  • Strong maritime influence from False Bay
  • Ideal for aromatic whites and elegant reds

Specialties:

  • Sauvignon Blanc - crisp, elegant style
  • Premium Chardonnay
  • Historic: Sweet Muscat-based Vin de Constance

Swartland District

Inland region north of Cape Town

Climate:

Warm to hot - less maritime influence

Revolution in Quality:

  • Previously bulk wine region
  • Now: exciting quality wines from old bush vines
  • Natural/minimal intervention movement

Specialties:

  • Syrah - rich, full-bodied
  • Old-vine Chenin Blanc - complex, textured
