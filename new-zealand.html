New Zealand's largest wine region (2/3 of national production). World-famous for Sauvignon Blanc.

üçá Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc - Global Benchmark

Style characteristics:

High acidity, intense aromatics, pungent, vibrant

Flavor profile (distinctive):

  • Herbaceous: Gooseberry, green bell pepper, jalape√±o, grass, nettles
  • Tropical: Passion fruit, grapefruit, lime
  • Pungent aromatics: Cat's pee (thiols - positive descriptor!), elderflower

Winemaking:

Stainless steel fermentation (cool temp), no oak, no MLF. Goal: preserve intense fruit aromatics and acidity. Some producers use neutral barrel fermentation for texture.

Quality & Recognition:

Put New Zealand on wine map (1980s international breakthrough). Immediately identifiable style, worldwide demand. Ranges from commercial to premium.

Aging: Drink young (1-3 years) for primary fruit. Premium examples can age 3-5 years.

Other Marlborough Wines

Pinot Noir: Light to medium body, red fruit (cherry, cranberry), herbal notes. Quality improving, cooler sub-regions show promise.

Chardonnay: Citrus, white peach, high acidity. Unoaked to lightly oaked styles.

Pinot Gris: Off-dry to dry, medium body, pear, stone fruit. Popular domestic market.

Riesling: Dry to off-dry, lime, high acidity, mineral. High quality, underrated.

Sparkling wines: Traditional method, Chardonnay/Pinot Noir base. Quality improving significantly.

üçá Central Otago Pinot Noir - World-Class

Style: Medium to full body (riper than most NZ Pinot), high acidity (diurnal range), soft velvety tannins, vibrant

Flavor profile:

Red and black cherry, plum, floral (violet), spice, herbal complexity. Ripe but not jammy. Pure fruit expression.

Why it works:

  • Continental climate = full ripening
  • Large diurnal range = acidity retention
  • Intense sunshine = flavor development
  • Cool nights = aromatics, freshness

International recognition: Central Otago Pinot Noir competes with Burgundy and Oregon. Distinctive, pure, powerful yet elegant style.

Aging: 5-10 years for premium examples, up to 15 years for best wines

Central Otago Sub-Regions

Gibbston: Coolest, highest elevation. Aromatic, elegant Pinot Noir.

Bannockburn: Warmest, low elevation. Riper, fuller-bodied Pinot.

Bendigo: Warm, dry. Powerful, concentrated Pinot Noir.

Wanaka: Cool, high altitude. Fresh, aromatic wines.

Other New Zealand Regions

Hawke's Bay (North Island)

Climate: Warmer, moderate maritime

Speciality: Bordeaux blends (Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc). Full body, ripe fruit, structured. Highest-quality red wine region in NZ.

Also: Chardonnay (full, oaked style), Syrah emerging

Martinborough / Wairarapa (North Island)

Climate: Cool, dry, continental influence

Speciality: Pinot Noir - elegant, earthy, Burgundian style. Small production, boutique, high quality.

Also: Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Gris

Gisborne (North Island)

Climate: Warm, high rainfall

Wines: Chardonnay (full, tropical), aromatic whites. High-volume production, good value wines.

Nelson (South Island)

Climate: Cool, sunny, dry

Wines: Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Noir, Chardonnay. Similar to Marlborough but smaller, more diverse.

Canterbury / Waipara Valley (South Island)

Climate: Cool, dry (rain shadow)

Wines: Pinot Noir (elegant), Riesling (dry, aromatic), Sauvignon Blanc. Emerging quality region.
