Overview

Greece's most interesting wines arguably come from the country's many indigenous varieties. These grapes are well-adapted to survive in the arid, hot conditions typical of much of Greece. Over 200 native grape varieties, very few grown elsewhere.

Black Grape Varieties

Xinomavro

Greece's most acclaimed red variety

Best from: Naoussa PDO (northern Greece/Macedonia)

Characteristics:

  • High tannin and high acidity
  • Medium color - can be deceptive
  • Complex: Red fruit, spice, earthy notes
  • Often compared to Nebbiolo in structure
  • Excellent aging potential

Agiorgitiko

Best from: Nemea PDO (northern Peloponnese)

Characteristics:

  • Deep color
  • Soft, smooth tannins
  • Red fruit flavors (cherry, strawberry)
  • Sweet spice notes
  • More approachable young than Xinomavro
  • Versatile - various styles from ros√© to full-bodied reds

Key Wine Regions

Naoussa PDO

Location: Northern Greece (Macedonia region)

Grape: 100% Xinomavro

Geography:

  • Vineyards at altitudes up to 400m
  • Slopes provide good drainage and sun exposure
  • Altitude moderates summer heat

Wine Style:

  • High tannin, high acidity - structured wines
  • Medium color despite intensity
  • Complex aromas: red fruit, tomato, olive, spice
  • Needs bottle age to soften - premium wines age decades

Nemea PDO

Location: Northern Peloponnese

Grape: 100% Agiorgitiko

Geography:

  • Close to narrow strip of land joining peninsula to mainland
  • Range of altitudes - affects wine style
  • Lower altitude: Fuller, riper wines
  • Higher altitude: More acidity, fresher style

Wine Styles:

  • Deep color
  • Soft tannins make wines approachable young
  • Red fruit (cherry, strawberry)
  • Sweet spice notes
  • Range from easy-drinking to age-worthy premium wines

Santorini PDO

Location: Volcanic island in Aegean Sea

Primary Grape: Assyrtiko (minimum 75%)

Unique Viticulture:

Kouloura Training System

Basket-shaped vine training unique to Santorini

Why: Protects grapes from strong winds

Vines woven into low circular baskets on ground

Grapes grow protected inside basket

Volcanic Terroir:

  • Volcanic soils - porous, well-draining
  • Very low rainfall - vines rely on morning dew
  • Ungrafted vines - volcanic soil protected from phylloxera
  • Some vines extremely old (100+ years)

Wine Style:

  • Dry, full-bodied whites
  • High acidity - refreshing despite ripeness
  • Concentrated citrus and stone fruit
  • Distinctive minerality from volcanic soils
  • Can age beautifully

Sweet Wines:

Vinsanto (not to be confused with Italian Vin Santo)

Sweet wine made from sun-dried grapes

Minimum 2 years oak aging

Rich, concentrated, oxidative style

Amber color, dried fruit, nuts, caramel flavors

Greek Wine Classification

EU Classification System

Wine laws of Greece follow those of other EU countries:

PDO (Protected Designation of Origin):

  • Highest quality tier
  • Strict regulations on varieties, yields, winemaking
  • Examples: Naoussa, Nemea, Santorini

PGI (Protected Geographical Indication):

  • Regional wines with geographical link
  • More flexibility in varieties and winemaking

Table Wine:

  • No geographical indication
  • Basic quality tier
