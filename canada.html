Production Requirements:

VQA Icewine standards (strictest in world):

  • Temperature: Grapes must be naturally frozen on vine at -8¬∞C (17.6¬∞F) or colder
  • Harvest: Picked and pressed while frozen (usually 2-4am when coldest)
  • Process: Water stays frozen, concentrated juice extracted
  • No artificial freezing: Must be natural freeze (unlike some other countries)
  • Minimum sugar: 35¬∞ Brix at harvest

Wine Styles:

Riesling Icewine:

Medium to full body, very high acidity (balances intense sweetness), apricot, peach, honey, candied citrus peel. Elegant, complex. Ages 10-20+ years.

Vidal Icewine:

Vidal is a French-American hybrid. Fuller body than Riesling, slightly less acidity, tropical fruit, mango, lychee, honey. Rich, luscious. Easier to produce (hardier vines).

Cabernet Franc Icewine (Red):

Rare. Sweet red, strawberry, raspberry jam, dried fruit. Complex, unusual style.

Why Canada Dominates:

  • Reliable cold winters (-8¬∞C guaranteed most years)
  • Strictest quality regulations in world
  • Decades of expertise in production
  • Perfect climate for consistent quality

Serving: Well-chilled (6-8¬∞C). Small portions (50-75ml) due to intensity and sweetness.

Table Wines from Niagara

Riesling: Dry to off-dry, high acidity, citrus, stone fruit, mineral. Cool-climate, German-influenced style. Good quality.

Chardonnay: Medium body, citrus, stone fruit, some oak aging. Cool-climate, crisp style.

Cabernet Franc: Medium body, red fruit, herbal, peppery. Most successful red variety in cool Ontario climate.

Pinot Noir: Light to medium body, red fruit, elegant. Cool-climate style, quality improving.

Sub-Regions of Niagara Peninsula

Niagara-on-the-Lake: Coolest, closest to lake. Premium Icewine, Riesling, Chardonnay.

Niagara Escarpment: Bench land, warmer mesoclimate. Red varieties (Cabernet Franc, Pinot Noir).

Short Hills Bench: Protected, warmer. Red varieties, full-bodied wines.

Other Ontario Regions

Prince Edward County VQA: Cool, limestone soils. Chardonnay, Pinot Noir. Emerging quality region, boutique producers.

Lake Erie North Shore: Warmest Ontario region. Bordeaux varieties, fuller-bodied reds.

British Columbia

Okanagan Valley VQA

üìç Location

Interior BC, 150km north-south valley, Okanagan Lake

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Continental with warm, dry summers (rain shadow of mountains). Large diurnal range. Diverse mesoclimates.

üçá Varieties

Wide range: Pinot Noir, Merlot, Cabernet, Syrah, Chardonnay, Pinot Gris, Riesling

Wine Styles

Pinot Noir: Light to medium body, elegant, red fruit, good quality. Cool northern sites ideal.

Merlot & Cabernet Sauvignon: Medium to full body from warmer southern sites. Ripe fruit, structured.

Syrah: Full body, black fruit, pepper. Warmer sites. Gaining recognition.

Chardonnay: Range of styles from crisp/unoaked to rich/oaked depending on site.

Pinot Gris: Medium body, stone fruit, popular white variety.

Riesling: Dry to sweet styles, high acidity, citrus, stone fruit. Some Icewine production.

Climate Diversity

Northern Okanagan: Cooler, lake influence. Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, aromatic whites.

Central Okanagan: Moderate. Widest variety of grapes.

Southern Okanagan (Osoyoos): Warmest, desert-like. Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah thrive. Canada's warmest wine region.

Other BC Regions

Similkameen Valley VQA: East of Okanagan, warmer, drier. Organic viticulture common. Bordeaux varieties, Syrah.

Fraser Valley VQA: Coastal, cool, wet. Small production, aromatic varieties, fruit wines.

Vancouver Island VQA: Cool, maritime. Pinot Noir, Pinot Gris, sparkling wines.
