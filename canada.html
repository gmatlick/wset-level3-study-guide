World-renowned Icewine and elegant cool-climate wines

Canada's most important vineyard regions are in Ontario and British Columbia. The wine regions experience a harsh continental climate moderated by large bodies of water. Canada produces world-renowned Icewine along with steadily growing volumes of dry wines, both white and red.

Vintners Quality Alliance (VQA)

Canada's appellation system. Two provinces currently use VQA: Ontario and British Columbia. Each province has approved appellations and sub-appellations.

Climate

Continental throughout Canada's wine regions. Low average annual temperatures, but average daytime temps in July-August are very high. Large lakes moderate temperature extremes. Winters are cold, temperatures far below zero for long periods, creating suitable conditions for Icewine production.

Ontario

Niagara Peninsula (Most Important Appellation)

SW corner of Lake Ontario. Lake cools/heats more slowly than surrounding land. This extends growing season in autumn (full ripening) and delays budburst in spring (minimizes frost damage). Air flow reduces fungal disease.

üçá White Varieties

Riesling: Does well. Fresh, fruity dry and off-dry whites. Finest Icewines.

Vidal (hybrid): Hardy. Commonly used for Icewine. Little character when dry, but makes high-quality Icewine (though without acid structure and aromatic complexity of Riesling).

Chardonnay: Good quality dry wines, generally pure fruit with restrained oak.

üçá Red Varieties

Cabernet Franc (most planted black): Red plum and herbal characteristics. Single varietal or Bordeaux-style blends with Merlot and Cabernet Sauvignon.

Pinot Noir: Small plantings but some high-quality wines being made.

Icewine Specialty

Ontario is world-famous for Icewine. Grapes left on vine through winter, picked frozen at -7¬∞C minimum. Concentrated sweet wines with high acidity. Primarily Riesling (finest quality, complex aromatics) or Vidal (hardy, high quality but less complex).

British Columbia

Okanagan Valley (Most Important Appellation)

Huge appellation. Rain shadow between two mountain ranges = very low annual rainfall, semi-desert environment. Extremely long day lengths due to northern latitude prolongs photosynthesis, aids grape ripening. Large glacial lakes moderate temperatures and extend growing season. North: Successful white grape ripening. South: Black grape ripening.
