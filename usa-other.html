Cool-climate excellence beyond California

Oregon

Willamette Valley AVA

üìç Location

Northwest Oregon, south of Portland, between Coast Range and Cascade Mountains

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Cool, moderate rainfall (unlike California). More similar to Burgundy climate than rest of USA.

üçá Focus

Pinot Noir - the signature grape of Oregon

üç∑ Oregon Pinot Noir Style

Medium body, elegant, high acidity, red fruit (cherry, raspberry, cranberry), earthy, mushroom, forest floor. Less oaky than California, more restrained, Burgundian influence strong. Silky tannins.

Quality: World-class. Oregon Pinot Noir competes with Burgundy at international tastings.

Aging: 5-15 years for premium examples

Oregon Wine Laws (Stricter than Federal)

Varietal labeling: 90% minimum (vs 75% federal) for Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, most varieties

AVA labeling: 95% minimum from stated AVA (vs 85% federal)

Reflects: Quality-focused, Burgundian approach, sustainability emphasis

Willamette Valley Sub-AVAs

Dundee Hills: Volcanic soils, elegant Pinot Noir

Yamhill-Carlton: Sedimentary soils, structured Pinot

Eola-Amity Hills: Volcanic, powerful Pinot Noir

Ribbon Ridge: Small, high-quality Pinot specialist

Other Oregon Wines

Chardonnay: Cool-climate, high acid, lean, mineral. Less oaky than California.

Pinot Gris: Off-dry to dry, medium body, stone fruit. Popular Oregon white.

Southern Oregon: Warmer regions (Rogue Valley, Umpqua Valley) - Bordeaux varieties, Syrah emerging.

Washington State

Columbia Valley AVA

üìç Location

Eastern Washington (and small part of Oregon), inland, rain shadow of Cascade Mountains

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Continental, hot days, cool nights (large diurnal range), very low rainfall (desert - irrigation essential), long daylight hours in summer (northern latitude)

üçá Key Grapes

Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Syrah, Riesling, Chardonnay

üç∑ Washington Wine Styles

Cabernet Sauvignon & Merlot: Full body, ripe but balanced (diurnal range preserves acidity), concentrated black fruit, oak influence, structured. Very good quality, better value than California.

Syrah: Full body, black fruit, pepper, meaty, spice. Dark, concentrated. Increasingly important variety.

Riesling: Ranges from bone-dry to sweet. High acidity, pure fruit, citrus, stone fruit. Excellent quality, underrated.

Important Sub-AVAs

Yakima Valley AVA: Warm, within Columbia Valley. Cabernet, Merlot, Syrah. Long-established quality region.
Walla Walla Valley AVA: Warmer, crosses into Oregon. Premium Cabernet and Syrah. Boutique wineries, high quality.
Horse Heaven Hills AVA: Large, warm. Consistent ripening, good quality Cab/Merlot.

Washington Characteristics

Advantages: Consistent weather (little vintage variation), disease pressure low (dry), long daylight hours, excellent acidity retention (diurnal range)

Challenges: Winter freeze risk can damage vines, irrigation infrastructure required

Quality trend: Rapidly improving, gaining international recognition, excellent value

New York State

Finger Lakes AVA

üìç Location

Upstate New York, series of deep glacial lakes

üå§Ô∏è Climate

Cool continental, lake effect moderates temperature, extends growing season, reduces frost risk. Cold winters.

üçá Speciality

Riesling - the star variety of Finger Lakes

üç∑ Finger Lakes Riesling

Styles range from: Bone dry to lusciously sweet

Dry Riesling: High acidity, citrus (lime, lemon), green apple, mineral, steely. Crisp, refreshing. Can age 5-10 years.

Off-dry/Sweet Riesling: Balanced sweetness with high acidity, stone fruit, honey. Late harvest styles common.

Ice Wine: Cold winters perfect for ice wine production. Intense sweetness, high acidity, apricot, honey. World-class quality.

Quality level: Finger Lakes Riesling competes with Germany and Alsace. Underrated internationally but gaining recognition.

Other Finger Lakes Varieties

Cabernet Franc: Most successful red variety. Medium body, herbal, red fruit. Cool-climate style.

Chardonnay & Pinot Noir: Emerging quality, still developing

Hybrid varieties: Still planted (Vidal, Seyval Blanc) for harsh winters, but Vitis vinifera increasing

Long Island AVAs

Location: Eastern Long Island (North Fork, Hamptons)

Climate: Moderate maritime, Atlantic influence

Wines: Bordeaux varieties (Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc), Chardonnay. Medium body, balanced, elegant style. Small but growing region.
